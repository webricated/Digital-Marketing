Chapter 1.2: How to Move Prospects Through the Customer Value Journey

Now that you understand what the Customer Value Journey is, the next big question is simple:

How do you smoothly guide people from one stage to the next?

The answer lies in creating marketing campaigns that are designed with clear intent. Nothing is left to chance.

Marketing Campaigns: The Engine of Movement

A marketing campaign exists for one purpose—to move people forward in the journey. Whether that means turning awareness into engagement or customers into loyal fans, campaigns make progress happen.

What Exactly Is a Marketing Campaign?

Every effective campaign has two essential elements:

  • A Call to Action (CTA) – what you want the audience to do
  • A Traffic Source – where those people come from

The call to action depends on the stage you’re targeting:

  • Awareness: Read a blog, watch a video, listen to a podcast
  • Subscribe: Download a checklist, webinar, or guide
  • Convert / Ascend: Purchase a product or service

Traffic may come from ads, email, search engines, social media, or even offline channels like print or radio.

A campaign is successful only if it moves someone from one specific stage to the very next stage.

Why “Intentional” Marketing Matters

People often move through the Customer Value Journey naturally—even without a company planning for it.

For example, a friend casually recommending a tool can create awareness or even promotion. But relying on chance limits growth.

Now compare that with a referral program that rewards users for inviting others. In this case, movement through the journey is carefully engineered.

Intentional campaigns give you control over growth by fixing the stages where prospects tend to get stuck.

The #1 Campaign Mistake Businesses Make

Once companies understand the journey, they often try to do too much—too fast.

Trying to turn a stranger into a promoter with one campaign simply doesn’t work.

Instead, effective marketers create focused campaigns that move people one step at a time.

Real-World Campaign Examples

Case Study 1: Awareness → Engagement

  • Goal: Get people to engage
  • Content: Branding video
  • Traffic: Facebook video ads
  • CTA: “Watch this video”

The campaign focused purely on attention and engagement—no selling, no sign-ups. Simple focus led to strong results.

Case Study 2: Engagement → Subscribe

  • Goal: Build an email list
  • Content: Helpful blog post
  • Traffic: Organic social media
  • CTA: Email sign-up popup

This campaign quietly converts engaged readers into subscribers using a clear, low-friction call to action.

Case Study 3: Engage → Subscribe → Convert

  • Goal: First-time purchase
  • Traffic: Search ads
  • CTA: Limited-time discount

By offering a first-order incentive, this campaign reduces hesitation and accelerates conversion.

The best campaigns are often the simplest—clear goal, clear message, clear next step.

Key Takeaway

The Customer Value Journey is the foundation of every digital marketing strategy. Each campaign should exist to move people forward—nothing more, nothing less.

As you continue through this guide, you’ll see how every tactic connects back to this journey.
